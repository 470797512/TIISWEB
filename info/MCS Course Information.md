## 1. Course Overview

The Master of Cyber Security (MCS) at The Institute of International Studies (TIIS) is a postgraduate qualification designed to develop advanced knowledge, technical capability, and professional expertise in cyber security.

The program integrates:

- foundation IT knowledge (for non-IT entrants)

- core cyber security disciplines

- business and governance capability

- capstone project or internship

to ensure graduates are industry-ready, technically competent, and capable of managing cyber security challenges in complex organisational environments .

The course aligns with:

- Cyber Security Body of Knowledge (CyBOK)

- Australian Computer Society (ACS) expectations

- Industry demand for cyber security professionals

### Course Details

- AQF Level: 9 (Masters Degree – Coursework)

- CRICOS Code: *115211M*

- Duration: 2 years full-time (or part-time equivalent) \*

- Delivery Mode: Face-to-Face, Online and Hybrid (Blended)\*\*

- Campus: Sydney & Melbourne

- Intakes: Multiple intakes per year

\* Duration may vary depending on enrolment load and progression.  
\*\* Delivery modes are subject to regulatory approval, course availability, and visa conditions.

## 3. Course Structure

The MCS consists of 16 subjects (96 credit points) structured into five integrated components:

- 4 Foundation IT Units

- 3 Business Administration Units

- 6 Core Cyber Security Units

- 2 Cyber Security Electives

- 1 Capstone Unit (Project or Internship)

This structure supports:

- entry from non-IT backgrounds

- progressive development from foundation → advanced levels

- alignment with industry and professional standards

## 5. Learning Outcomes

Graduates of the MCS will be able to:

- Apply advanced cyber security knowledge to design and manage secure systems

- Analyse and evaluate cyber security risks, threats, and governance frameworks

- Develop technical and strategic solutions to cyber security challenges

- Communicate effectively with technical and non-technical stakeholders

- Apply ethical, regulatory, and global perspectives in cyber security practice

- Deliver industry-relevant solutions through independent or collaborative projects

## 6. Delivery Mode and Study Requirements

Delivery Modes

- Face-to-Face: On-campus structured classes

- Online: LMS-supported delivery with virtual learning

- Hybrid: Combination of on-campus and online learning

All delivery modes ensure:

- equivalent learning outcomes

- consistent assessment standards

- full student support

⚠️ International Students: Online study may be restricted under visa conditions.

Technology Requirements

Students are expected to have access to:

- A high-performance computer (minimum 16GB RAM recommended)

- Reliable high-speed internet connection

- Ability to run virtualisation and specialised cyber security tools

Some software may require Windows-based environments

## 7. Entry Requirements

Academic Requirements

Applicants must have:

- A recognised Bachelor degree (any discipline), OR

- A Graduate Certificate or Diploma

Credit for Prior Learning (CPL) may apply for:

- IT-related qualifications (foundation units)

- business-related qualifications (business units)

English Language Requirements

- IELTS 6.5 overall (no band below 6.0), or equivalent
