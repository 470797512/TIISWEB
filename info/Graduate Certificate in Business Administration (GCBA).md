## 1. Course Overview

The Graduate Certificate in Business Administration (GCBA) at The Institute of International Studies (TIIS) is a postgraduate qualification designed to provide foundational business knowledge and practical management skills across key functional areas.

The program equips students with:

- essential understanding of accounting, economics, marketing, and management

- practical skills to address real-world business challenges

- the ability to transition into leadership roles or further postgraduate study

The GCBA is particularly suited for:

- professionals seeking to upskill or transition into business roles

- newly promoted managers requiring formal business knowledge

- students intending to pathway into the MBA program

Course Details

- AQF Level: 8 (Graduate Certificate)

- CRICOS Code: 107618K

- Duration: Approximately 16–26 weeks full-time (or part-time equivalent) \*

- Number of Subjects: 4

- Delivery Mode: Face-to-Face, Online and Hybrid (Blended) \*\*

- Campus: Sydney and Melbourne

<!-- -->

- Intakes: Multiple intakes per year

\* Duration may vary depending on enrolment load and progression.  
\*\* Delivery modes are subject to regulatory approval, course availability, and visa conditions.

## 3. Course Structure

The GCBA consists of 4 core subjects (24 credit points).

The program is designed as:

- a standalone qualification, OR

- a pathway into the MBA, with full credit transfer

The structure aligns with the first stage of the MBA, ensuring a smooth transition into further postgraduate study .

## 4. Subject Structure

4.1 Core Units (4 Units)

These units provide a strong foundation in business and management.

| Unit Code | Unit Name                         |
|-----------|-----------------------------------|
| MBA602    | Marketing Management              |
| MBA601    | Accounting for Managers           |
| MBA603    | Management, Leadership and Ethics |
| MPA503    | Economics for Business            |

## 5. Learning Outcomes

Graduates of the GCBA will be able to:

Knowledge

- Demonstrate understanding of key business disciplines including:

  - accounting

  - economics

  - marketing

  - management

Skills

- Critically evaluate management theories and apply them in business contexts

- Identify and implement solutions to business challenges

- Integrate theoretical and practical business knowledge

- Communicate business concepts effectively to professional audiences

These outcomes prepare graduates to operate effectively in contemporary business environments .
