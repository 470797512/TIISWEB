## 1. Course Overview

The Graduate Diploma of Financial Planning (GDFP) at The Institute of International Studies (TIIS) is a postgraduate qualification designed to develop advanced knowledge, analytical capability, and professional skills in financial planning and advisory practice.

The program prepares graduates to:

- identify and manage complex financial planning challenges

- apply professional judgement in investment, taxation, superannuation, and risk management

- operate effectively within regulated financial services environments

The GDFP is aligned with professional standards for financial advisers in Australia, supporting graduates to meet educational requirements for industry practice .

Course Details

- AQF Level: 8 (Graduate Diploma)

- Duration: Up to 16 months full-time (or part-time equivalent)\*

- Delivery Mode: Face-to-Face, Online and Hybrid (Blended)\*\*

- Campus: Sydney and Melbourne

- Intakes: Multiple intakes per year

\* Duration may vary depending on enrolment load and progression.

\*\* Delivery modes are subject to regulatory approval, course availability, and visa conditions.

## 3. Course Structure

The GDFP consists of 8 core subjects (48 credit points), including:

- 7 Core Financial Planning Units

- 1 Capstone Unit (Financial Plan Construction)

The course is designed to progressively develop:

- foundational financial knowledge

- applied financial planning capability

- advanced advisory and professional practice skills

## 4. Subject Structure

### 4.1 Core Units (8 Units)

| Unit Code | Unit Name                                      |
|-----------|------------------------------------------------|
| FP501     | Principles of Taxation                         |
| FP502     | Superannuation and Retirement Planning         |
| FP503     | Principles of Investment                       |
| FP504     | Insurance, Risk Management and Estate Planning |
| FP505     | Ethics of Financial Planning                   |
| FP506     | Commercial Law and Financial Advice            |
| FP507     | Behavioural Finance                            |
| FP508     | Financial Plan Construction (Capstone)         |

### 4.2 Capstone Unit

| Unit Code | Unit Name                   |
|-----------|-----------------------------|
| FP508     | Financial Plan Construction |

The capstone integrates knowledge across all subject areas and enables students to:

- develop comprehensive financial plans

- apply technical, ethical, and regulatory frameworks

- demonstrate professional competency in real-world scenarios

## 5. Learning Outcomes

Graduates of the GDFP will be able to:

- Apply advanced financial planning knowledge in complex contexts

- Analyse and interpret client financial situations and objectives

- Develop compliant and effective financial strategies

- Apply ethical, legal, and regulatory frameworks in practice

- Communicate financial advice clearly to clients and stakeholders

## 7. Entry Requirements

Academic Requirements

Applicants must have:

- A recognised Bachelor degree (or equivalent), OR

- A Graduate Certificate in a finance-related field, OR

- A Diploma/Advanced Diploma in a finance-related field plus relevant industry experience

English Language Requirements

Students must demonstrate:

- IELTS 6.5 overall (or equivalent), OR

- Prior study or work experience in English-speaking environments (as per TIIS policy)
