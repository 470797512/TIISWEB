## 1. Course Overview

The Graduate Certificate in Professional Accounting (GCPA) at The Institute of International Studies (TIIS) is a postgraduate qualification designed to provide foundational accounting knowledge and practical financial skills for entry-level professional practice.

The program equips students with:

- essential knowledge in financial accounting, business law, economics, and finance

- the ability to analyse and interpret financial and business information

- practical capability to operate in entry-level accounting and finance roles

The GCPA is designed as:

- a standalone qualification, OR

- a pathway into the Master of Professional Accounting (Advanced) (MPAA)

## 4. Course Structure

The GCPA consists of 4 core subjects (24 credit points).

The program is:

- an entry-level postgraduate qualification, and

- nested within the MPA and MPAA programs, aligned with the first stage of those degrees

This enables students to:

- exit with a qualification after completing four subjects, OR

- progress into a full Master-level accounting program

## 5. Subject Structure

### 5.1 Core Units (4 Units)

| Unit Code | Unit Name                |
|-----------|--------------------------|
| MPA501    | Accounting Principles    |
| MPA502    | Business and Company Law |
| MPA503    | Economics for Business   |
| MPA504    | Business Finance         |

These subjects provide foundational capability across:

- financial accounting principles

- legal and regulatory frameworks

- economic decision-making

- financial management

## 6. Learning Outcomes

Graduates of the GCPA will be able to:

Knowledge

- Demonstrate understanding of core accounting, legal, and financial principles

- Apply knowledge of Australian business and regulatory frameworks

Skills

- Analyse financial data and business scenarios

- Apply accounting and finance concepts to practical problems

- Interpret and communicate financial information effectively

- Apply ethical and professional judgement in business contexts

These outcomes prepare graduates to operate in entry-level professional accounting environments
