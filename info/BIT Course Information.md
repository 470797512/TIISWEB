## 1. Course Overview

The Bachelor of Information Technology (BIT) at The Institute of International Studies (TIIS) is an undergraduate qualification designed to develop broad and coherent knowledge and practical skills in Information and Communication Technology (ICT).

The program prepares graduates for professional roles in:

- software and application development

- networking and systems administration

- web and mobile technologies

- cyber security and digital systems

The course integrates technical capability, problem-solving skills, teamwork, and ethical practice, ensuring graduates are industry-ready and adaptable to evolving technological environments .

Course Details

- AQF Level: 7 (Bachelor Degree)

- CRICOS Code: *(113724B)*

- Duration: 156 weeks (approximately 3 years full-time) (or part-time equivalent) \*

- Delivery Mode: Face-to-Face (on-campus), Online Delivery, Hybrid (Blended) \*\*

- Campus: Sydney & Melbourne

- Intakes: Multiple intakes per year

\* Duration may vary depending on enrolment load and progression.  
\*\* Delivery modes are subject to regulatory approval, course availability, and visa conditions.

## 3. Course Structure

The BIT consists of 24 subjects (144 credit points), including:

- 17 Core Subjects (Compulsory)

- 7 Specialisation Subjects (selected from available streams)

Students develop knowledge progressively across:

- IT foundations

- software development

- networking and cyber security

- data analytics and cloud computing

- applied project work (capstone)

## 5. Learning Outcomes

Graduates of the BIT will be able to:

- Demonstrate broad and coherent knowledge of IT systems and technologies

- Analyse and solve complex technical problems

- Design and implement IT solutions

- Communicate effectively in professional contexts

- Work independently and collaboratively in teams

- Apply ethical and regulatory frameworks in IT practice

## 6. Delivery Mode and Study Requirements

<span id="_Toc227486886" class="anchor"></span>The Bachelor of Information Technology (BIT) is delivered through a combination of flexible study modes to support diverse student needs and learning preferences:

- Face-to-Face Delivery:  
  On-campus classes in Sydney, delivered in structured sessions to support interactive learning and direct engagement with academic staff

- Online Delivery:  
  Delivered via the TIIS Learning Management System (LMS), including recorded lectures, live virtual classes, online discussions, and digital assessments

- Hybrid (Blended) Delivery:  
  A combination of on-campus and online learning activities, providing flexibility while maintaining academic engagement

- All delivery modes are designed to ensure equivalent learning outcomes, assessment standards, and student support, in line with regulatory requirements.

- Important (International Students):  
  Delivery modes are subject to visa conditions and regulatory requirements under the ESOS Act and National Code.

- 7\. Entry Requirements

### Academic Requirements

Applicants must meet one of the following:

- Completion of Year 12 (ATAR ≥ 60) with Mathematics

- AQF Level 5 Diploma or higher

- Recognised bridging or enabling program

Alternative entry pathways may be considered under TIIS Admissions Policy

### English Language Requirements

- IELTS 6.0 overall (no band below 6.0), or equivalent
